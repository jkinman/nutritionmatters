Introduction

Brixton is a Ghost Blog Theme in elegant and clean design. It is perfect for any personal blog. It follows the trendy hipster design with a combination of pure elegance. With Brixton you get right to the point, no unnecessary Slideshows and animations, just the information that you need, presented in a clean and minimal way. With Instagram and Twitter widgets all your main social medias are covered with no extra hassle.

We wish you to have success and smooth blogging experience with Brixton theme!

Installation

Installing Ghost

I guess you already have Ghost installed? If not then when installing Ghost, you'll need to have Node.js installed. Don't go running for the hills, node will make this whole process really easy.

Official documentation for installing Ghost: http://support.ghost.org/installation/

Of course, there are numerious articles or video tutorials all over the internet of how to properly install Ghost, also lately there are lot of hosting companies who offer installation of Ghost with one click.

Installing Brixton Ghost theme

Once you have successfully installed your Ghost blogging platform, you are ready to install and activate Brixton theme.

It is very easy to do it, first and important is to extract the zip file to your desktop and upload Brixton theme via ftp to the content/themes folder. After uploading Brixton to your server go to your admin dashboard, the url will be something like this: http://yourghostblog.com/ghost, once you access your dashboard simply click on General tab or precisely http://yourghostblog/ghost/settings/general/ and select the Brixton theme from the dropdown. If Brixton does not appear in the dropdown you will have to restart your Ghost Blog.

Now head back to your home page and you will see your new Brixton theme up and running!

Header

Header consist of two partials, top-bar.hbs and header.hbs.

Top bar consist of two parts: navigation or left and social icons on right, it is all stored inside the file named top-bar.hbs located into partials/header folder.

To change the social links in header to link to your own social networks, simply open the file named top-bar.hbs with some text or code editor and find for example this line:

<li><a href="#"><i class="fa fa-twitter"></i></a></li>

and populate the href tag with your own link, so would be:

<li><a href="http://YourTwitterLink"><i class="fa fa-twitter"></i></a></li>

Note: The socials on top bar differ from the socials on posts. Socials on post share your article, they do not link to your own networks.

The logo of the theme is on the header and is displayed as image, it fetches the image from the "Blog Logo" uploaded via your admin dashboard.

Blog description is displayed as text. Once you populate the "Blog Description" from your dashboard it will show right below the logo image.

Featured

The top three posts are always featured posts, you can set featured post via (explain here where with screenshot), due to lack of ghost sticky posts, featured will only show on home page, that is why we made home.hbs template, despite index.hbs which displays without featured on every other page except homepage. You have to mark latest posts as featured, or other posts that will be in number of total posts, you can set how many posts per page you want to show via general tab. Make sure you always have maximum 3 items marked at featured.

Blogging

Disqus Comments

To use your own disqus, you only have to insert your disqus shortname that you already have from your disqus account, if you do not have already you can open a new one over at Disqus
Once you have your disqus shortname, please open the file post.hbs located into the root of the theme, and on line number 86 which reads:

var disqus_shortname = 'yourDisqusUsername' // required: replace example with your forum shortname
Between the "", add your own disqus shortname, and you are set to disquss.

Here is more information of how to get your shortname: https://help.disqus.com/customer/portal/articles/466208

Sidebar

For easy management, each widget has it's own file, they are separate, all are located into partials/widgets folder.

You can easily remove a widget by opening the sidebar.hbs file that is located into the partials folder. For example to remove the twitter widget you can comment this line like this// {{>widgets/twitter}} or simply delete it.

- About us widget -

This is a simple html code with image and text. Here we are not fetching the actual author image, but instead we are linking to a custom image, that way we give you opportunity to add whatever other image you want, you just need to replace the image in the images folder with your own image. But we are using the actual author image on single blog post, we did it like this because this way we can have 2 options instead of one.

 

- Twitter widget -

1. Go to www.twitter.com and sign in as normal, go to your settings page.
2. Go to "Widgets" on the left hand side.
3. Create a new widget for what you need eg "user timeline" or "search" etc.
4. Feel free to check "exclude replies" if you dont want replies in results.
5. Now go back to settings page, and then go back to widgets page, you should see the widget you just created. Click edit.
6. Now look at the URL in your web browser, you will see a long number like this: 345735908357048478
7. Open the twitter.js file located into the assets/js folder and insert/use this as your ID below instead!

- No sidebar? -

You do not need sidebar at all? Want full width blog? No problem, open the index.hbs file and change the following:

<main class="unit-70" role="main">

to

<main class="unit-100" role="main">

also scroll down a bit and remove the following lines:

        <aside class="unit-30">
            {{! The tag below includes the sidebar - partials/sidebar.hbs }}
            {{> "sidebar"}}
        </aside>

And you will have full blog page.

Footer

Footer consist of two partials, copyright.hbs and footer.hbs, both are located inside of partials/footer folder.

- Instagram in footer? -

In order to get this running you need to get your User ID and Access Token

1. Go to http://instagram.com/developer

2. Click on "Manage Clients" in top right corner.

3. Click on "Register a New Client".

4. Fill all the required fields

5. Click on "Register" and copy your "Client ID" and "Client Secret".

6. If you need to get your accessToken please add the following url in your browser address bar:

https://instagram.com/oauth/authorize/?client_id=[CLIENT_ID_HERE]&redirect_uri=http://localhost&response_type=token

and add the client id you generated to the url replacing "[CLIENT_ID_HERE]" (without quotes).

Hit enter, and you will connect to Instagram's servers. Instagram will ask you whether you want to grant the client you registered in Step 2 access to your account.Once your client has been granted access to your account, Instagram will redirect your browser to your localhost and your newly generated access token will be appended to the url after localhost/#access_token=

6. Open the default.hbs file with text/code editor and on the bottom you will find the javascript code that has all instagram info, fill in the userId and accessToken with your own.

- Alternative version -

We have also added an alternative, we always try to make easier for you, or at least have alternative choice :)

If you do not want to use this instagram widget and avoid getting the instagram ID, access tokens etc, simply open the footer.hbs located into partials folder and you will see line that says:

{{!-- INSTANSIVE WIDGET - UNCOMMENT THE LINE BELOW IF YOU WANT TO USE IFRAME AND AVOID LOGINS HASSLE --}}

Uncomment the script tags below that line and you are good to go. It is iframe ready widget that comes from this service: http://instansive.com
